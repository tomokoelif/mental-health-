Our Services

Zazen Classes

Zazen is a simple yet profound practice that combines posture, breathing, and mental awareness. The goal of zazen is to let go of judgment and goals, and to attain a state of union between the body and mind.

Shakyo

Shakyo involves concentrating and copying characters. By carefully copying, you can achieve a calm, unruffled state of mind. When you reach this state, the brain secretes a substance called beta-endorphin, which has a pain-relieving effect and gives a sense of happiness. This is why shakyo is called "writing meditation.

Takigyou

Waterfall training is known as a practice to get rid of worldly desires. In waterfall training, it is believed that these worldly desires are washed away by spending a certain amount of time in cold water. By continuing waterfall training, unnecessary emotions in the mind are purified and one can achieve mental peace.

Shinrinyoku Onsen

Forest bathing is a therapeutic practice that involves spending time in a forest or natural setting to connect with nature through mindful sensory engagement. And the effects of hot spring baths include recovery from fatigue and improved overall health.

Shoujin Ryouri

Shojin ryori developed as food for monks in Zen temples and was the cuisine they ate during training. In Buddhism, eating meat is basically prohibited, and Shojin ryori was created to avoid "slaughter (the killing of living things)" and to avoid stimulating "earthly desires (the minds that torment and trouble people)."

Sadou

The tea ceremony also has the effect of calming the mind while moving the body in a set manner. Brewing tea every day in a set manner allows you to experience various sensations with your five senses and become aware of subtle things outside of yourself.

日常五心-Nichijo Goshin

This is a traditional Zen Style Japanese teaching that shows five mindsets we should have in our daily lives.

  • 寛容-Kanyou-Tolerance: Be open-minded and accept the opinions and actions of others.
  • 反省-Hansei-Self-reflection: Reflect on your daily actions and behavior and identify areas for improvement.
  • 奉仕-Houshi-Service: Be mindful of serving others and valuing contributions to society.
  • 謙虚-Kenkyo-Humility: Treat others with humility and do not be overconfident.
  • 感謝-Kansha-Gratitude: Be grateful to others and your environment, and be thankful for the things you take for granted.
